Chapter 18 — Null Hypothesis Significance Testing

Topics covered: null and alternative hypotheses, test statistics, P-values, significance levels, Fisher's evidence view, Neyman-Pearson decision rules, the modern hybrid of both traditions, reject versus fail-to-reject language, Type I and Type II errors, one-sided and two-sided tests, confidence-interval duality, multiple testing and p-hacking, practical versus statistical significance, and final-style multiple-choice interpretation. The main exam traps are treating the P-value as \(P(H_0\mid\text{data})\), saying a nonsignificant result proves \(H_0\), choosing \(\alpha\) after seeing the data, confusing statistical and practical importance, and ignoring repeated testing.
