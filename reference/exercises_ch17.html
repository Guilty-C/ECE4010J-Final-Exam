Chapter 17 — Neyman-Pearson Decision Theory

Topics covered: null and alternative hypotheses, decision rules, Type I and Type II errors, significance level \(\alpha\), miss probability \(\beta\), power, critical regions, operating characteristic curves, sample-size planning, one-sided and two-sided decision tests, known-variance normal examples, Neyman-Pearson versus Fisher interpretation, confidence-interval duality, and final-style scenario diagnosis. The main exam traps are confusing \(\alpha\) with a posterior probability, treating a large \(p\)-value as proof of \(H_0\), forgetting that \(\beta\) depends on the true parameter value, using two-sided cutoffs for one-sided alternatives, and ignoring the pre-specified decision rule.
