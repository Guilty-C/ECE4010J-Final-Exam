Chapter 15 — Interval Estimation

Topics covered: interpretation of confidence intervals, centered two-sided intervals, one-sided bounds, known-variance Z intervals, unknown-variance T intervals, chi-squared intervals for variance and standard deviation, normality assumptions, sample-size design, and homework-style extensions to exponential and discrete-uniform models. The main exam traps are treating the parameter as random, using \(z\) when \(\sigma\) is unknown, reversing chi-squared quantiles, forgetting \(n-1\) degrees of freedom, and interpreting one-sided confidence bounds as two-sided intervals.
