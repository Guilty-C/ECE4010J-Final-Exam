Chapter 25 — Categorical Data

Topics covered: multinomial sampling, categorical likelihoods, chi-squared goodness-of-fit tests, degrees of freedom when parameters are estimated, binning continuous variables, expected counts, combining sparse categories, tests of independence and homogeneity in contingency tables, residual contributions, and final-style diagnosis. The main exam traps are using observed percentages as expected probabilities, forgetting estimated-parameter degrees of freedom, testing after data-dependent binning, interpreting association as causation, and running chi-squared approximations with too many small expected counts.
