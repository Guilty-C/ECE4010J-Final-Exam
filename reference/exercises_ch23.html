Chapter 23 — Comparison of Two Means

Topics covered: known-variance two-sample Z procedures, Student pooled T tests and intervals, Welch T tests and Welch-Satterthwaite degrees of freedom, one-sided confidence bounds for \(\mu_1-\mu_2\), sample-size and power planning, assumptions for independent samples, variance pre-test pitfalls, and final-style diagnosis of which two-mean method is appropriate. The recurring traps are pooling without a defensible common-variance model, choosing pooled vs. Welch after an F pre-test, using \(n_1+n_2-2\) degrees of freedom for Welch, and treating paired data as independent two-sample data.
