Chapter 21 — Inferences on Proportions

Topics covered: one-proportion confidence intervals, one-proportion hypothesis tests, large-sample normal approximation, validity conditions, conservative sample-size planning with \(p=1/2\), planning from a pilot estimate, interpretation of confidence intervals and P-values, and final-style diagnosis of common errors. The recurring exam traps are using \(\hat p\) instead of \(p_0\) in a null test standard error, treating the sample proportion as the parameter, ignoring success-failure conditions, failing to round sample sizes up, and interpreting a P-value as the probability that the null hypothesis is true.
