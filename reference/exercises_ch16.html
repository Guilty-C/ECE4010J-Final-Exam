Chapter 16 — The Fisher Test

Topics covered: Fisher's null hypothesis test, forming \(H_0\), one-tailed and two-tailed P-values, Z-test statistics for normal sample means with known variance, P-values as evidence against \(H_0\), rejection language, assumptions, confidence-interval/test duality, Bayesian/base-rate cautions, repeated evidence, and common final-exam pitfalls. The main traps are reversing the tail, treating a P-value as \(P(H_0\mid D)\), saying that failure to reject proves \(H_0\), doubling a one-sided P-value when the test is directional, and ignoring the model used to compute the test statistic.
