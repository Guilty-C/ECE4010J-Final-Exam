Chapter 30 — Multiple Linear Regression III: Finding the Right Model

Topics covered: categorical and indicator predictors, interaction indicators, separate versus combined regressions, partial \(F\)-tests, model selection, forward/backward/stepwise procedures, overfitting, multiple-testing traps, adjusted \(R^2\), validation thinking, PRESS / prediction sum of squares, and final-style model diagnosis. The main exam traps are interpreting dummy-variable intercepts incorrectly, forgetting hierarchy when interactions are present, selecting by ordinary \(R^2\) alone, trusting a model chosen after many unreported tests, and confusing in-sample residual error with prediction error.
