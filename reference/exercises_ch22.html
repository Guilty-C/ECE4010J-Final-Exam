Chapter 22 — Comparison of Two Variances

Topics covered: the F distribution as a ratio of independent chi-squared variables, reciprocal F critical values, one-sided and two-sided variance-ratio tests, confidence intervals for \(\sigma_1^2/\sigma_2^2\), normality and independence assumptions, OC/power caveats, the variance-equality pre-test caveat before two-sample mean tests, and final-style error diagnosis. Common traps are swapping numerator degrees of freedom, halving \(\alpha\) in a one-sided test, using a left-tail F table without the reciprocal identity, treating "fail to reject" as proof of equality, and pre-testing variances before choosing Student versus Welch.
