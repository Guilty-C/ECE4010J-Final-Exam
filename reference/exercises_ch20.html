Chapter 20 — Non-Parametric Single Sample Tests for the Median

Topics covered: hypotheses about a population median, exact and approximate sign tests, Wilcoxon signed-rank tests, one-sided and two-sided alternatives, zero differences, tied absolute differences, symmetry assumptions, robustness against outliers and skewness, and final-style method selection. The main exam traps are testing the mean instead of the median, forgetting to discard zero differences, using Wilcoxon without a symmetry assumption, mishandling tied ranks, and applying a normal approximation when an exact small-sample calculation is expected.
